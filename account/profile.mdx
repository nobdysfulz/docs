Your profile contains the personal and business information that PULSEIntel Pro uses across the platform — from how your AI agents address you to how your content is personalized.

## Viewing Your Profile

1. Click your **profile avatar** in the top-right corner of the screen
2. Select **Settings** from the dropdown menu
3. You'll land on the **Profile** tab by default

On mobile, tap the **Settings** icon in the bottom navigation bar.

## What's in Your Profile

### Personal Information
- **Name** — Your display name across the platform
- **Email** — The Google account email you used to sign in (this cannot be changed)
- **Phone number** — Used for RolePlay training calls and contact preferences
- **Profile photo** — Displayed in the top bar, AI agent conversations, and exported content

### Business Information
- **Brokerage name** — Your company or brokerage
- **Role** — Your position (agent, broker, team lead, etc.)
- **Years of experience** — Helps AI agents calibrate their advice
- **Specializations** — Your focus areas (residential, commercial, luxury, etc.)
- **Team information** — Team size and structure, if applicable

## How to Update Your Profile

1. Navigate to **Settings > Profile**
2. Click into any field to edit it
3. Make your changes
4. Click **Save** to update

<Tip>
Keeping your profile current helps your AI agents give you better, more personalized advice. If you change brokerages, move to a new market, or add a specialization, update your profile right away.
</Tip>

## Your Market Territory

Your market settings tell PULSEIntel Pro where you work, which powers:
- Localized content generation in the Content Studio
- Market intelligence data in your MORO score
- AI agent context when discussing market conditions

To update your market territory:
1. Go to **Settings > Market**
2. Update your city, state, zip codes, and market details
3. Click **Save**

## Profile Photo

To add or change your profile photo:
1. Go to **Settings > Profile**
2. Click on your current photo (or the placeholder icon)
3. Upload a new image from your device
4. Click **Save**

Your photo appears in the navigation bar, AI agent conversations, and any exported documents like PDF reports.
